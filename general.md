"*The core functions of traditional print map librarianship (i.e., identify, collect, organize, preserve and make available) can also be applied to digital geospatial resources (McEathron, 2001). It is assumed that certain map reading skills and cartographic knowledge will be obtained prior to or early in an individual’s job assignment. That knowledge will include scale, projection, grids, and geographic coordinates. It is also helpful to know the basics of the history of cartography; as well as local, state, federal and international mapping agencies and private map publishers, map series and similar publication patterns, and gazetteers (print and online)...The rapid expansion of online mapping technologies, many with simple interfaces popular among users, requires continually expanding skills that librarians obtain through reading, discussion and practice.*"


* Knowledge of geographic and cartographic principles, such as scale, projections, coordinate systems, and types of maps <small>*(Score: 3.56)*</small>
* Knowledge of authoritative references for information about cartographic resources <small>*(Score: 3.31)*</small>
* Knowledge of information and geospatial literacy standards for disciplines <small>*(Score: 2.89)*</small>
* Knowledge of creation and distribution systems for contemporary cartographic resources <small>*(Score: 2.87)*</small>
* Knowledge of how to use satellite imagery and aerial photography with indexes <small>*(Score: 2.62)*</small>
* Knowledge of the history of the production, publication, and distribution of cartographic information <small>*(Score: 2.42)*</small>
